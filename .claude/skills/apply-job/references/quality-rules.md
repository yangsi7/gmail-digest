# Quality Rules Reference

## Pre-Generation Checklist

Before writing, verify:
- [ ] Read cover-letter-context-guide.md
- [ ] Analyzed job description
- [ ] Classified job type
- [ ] Researched company (3+ specific details)
- [ ] Clarified with user (interests, achievements, tone)

---

## Style Rules (Non-Negotiable)

### Punctuation
- **NO em-dashes** (—)
- Use periods, commas, parentheses instead
- Use colons for lists

**Wrong**: "The product—which won a Red Dot Award—was sold out"
**Right**: "The product won a Red Dot Award. It sold out within months."

### Voice
- **Active voice 90%+**
- Avoid passive constructions

**Wrong**: "The team was led by me through the Series A"
**Right**: "I led the team through the Series A"

### Mood
- **NO subjunctive mood**
- Avoid: "would", "could", "might", "should"
- Use present or past tense

**Wrong**: "I would bring experience in health tech"
**Right**: "I bring experience in health tech"

### Sentence Length
- **Target: 10-20 words average**
- Mix short (8-12) and medium (15-20) sentences
- Never exceed 25 words

### Tone
- Professional, neutral, elegant
- Swiss formal (not casual startup)
- Confident without arrogance

## Vocabulary Blacklist

Never use these phrases:

### Category 1: Generic Openers (CRITICAL - auto-reject)

| Blacklisted | Why | Alternative |
|-------------|-----|-------------|
| "I am writing to apply" | Generic opener #1 | Company-specific hook |
| "I am excited to apply" | Generic opener #2 | Company-specific hook |
| "I noticed your job posting" | Shows no research | Reference specific company detail |
| "I am interested in the position" | Passive, weak | State what draws you to company |

### Category 2: Weak/Supplicant Language (HIGH)

| Blacklisted | Why | Alternative |
|-------------|-----|-------------|
| "I would be grateful" | Weak closing | "I look forward to discussing" |
| "I believe I can" | Uncertain | "I bring", "I have" |
| "Please consider" | Supplicant tone | Confident statement |
| "If given the opportunity" | Conditional, weak | Direct statement of intent |
| "I hope to" | Uncertain | "I look forward to" |

### Category 3: Empty Enthusiasm (MEDIUM)

| Blacklisted | Why | Alternative |
|-------------|-----|-------------|
| "passionate about" | Overused, vague | "I built", "I focus on" |
| "thrilled" | Overly emotional | Remove, be direct |
| "humbled" | False modesty | Remove entirely |
| "excited" | Overused | Remove, show through action |

### Category 4: Corporate Jargon (MEDIUM)

| Blacklisted | Why | Alternative |
|-------------|-----|-------------|
| "leverage my skills" | Corporate jargon | "apply", "bring" |
| "utilize" | Pompous | "use" |
| "synergy" | Corporate speak | Describe the actual benefit |
| "dynamic" | Meaningless | Be specific |
| "spearheaded" | Overused PM term | "led", "built", "launched" |
| "proven track record" | Empty without proof | State specific achievements |

### Category 5: Empty Claims (MEDIUM)

| Blacklisted | Why | Alternative |
|-------------|-----|-------------|
| "unique opportunity" | Cliché | Be specific about what's interesting |
| "team player" | Empty without evidence | Show collaboration with example |
| "detail-oriented" | Empty without evidence | Show attention to detail with example |
| "results-driven" | Empty adjective | State specific results |

## Vocabulary Whitelist

Preferred phrases:

| Use | When |
|-----|------|
| "I built" | Describing technical work |
| "I led" | Describing leadership |
| "I achieved" | Stating outcomes |
| "resulting in" | Connecting action to result |
| "measured by" | Introducing metrics |
| "demonstrated by" | Providing evidence |
| "I look forward to discussing" | Closing |
| "I am confident" | Expressing fit (sparingly) |

## Cover Letter Structure

### Length
- **350-450 words** (strict)
- 3-4 paragraphs (no section headers)
- Single page when printed

### Opening (50-70 words)
- Company-specific hook (NOT "I am writing to apply")
- Why this company interests you
- Your positioning statement

**Good opening**: "What draws me to [Company] is [specific thing about them]. This is exactly what I did at [relevant experience]."

### Body (200-280 words)
- 2-3 evidence paragraphs
- Each paragraph: Claim → Evidence → Result
- Include 2+ quantified achievements
- Include 3+ company-specific details

### Closing (50-70 words)
- Confidence (not gratitude)
- Specific next step
- No "I would be grateful"

**Good closing**: "I look forward to discussing how my experience in [X] can contribute to [Company's specific goal]."

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### 1. Generic Opening
**Wrong**: "I am writing to apply for the Product Manager position I saw on LinkedIn."
**Right**: "What draws me to RepRisk is your approach to human-in-the-loop risk data. I built similar systems at ISS-ESG."

### 2. Listing Without Evidence
**Wrong**: "I have strong leadership and communication skills."
**Right**: "I grew the product team from 1 to 8 people and led cross-functional hiring."

### 3. Restating the Resume
**Wrong**: "From 2022 to 2024, I was Head of Product at Oxa Life..."
**Right**: Use resume as evidence source, but tell a different story.

### 4. Weak Closing
**Wrong**: "Thank you for considering my application. I would be grateful for the opportunity."
**Right**: "I look forward to discussing how my experience can contribute to [specific company goal]."

### 5. Over-Explaining Gaps
**Wrong**: Long paragraphs about PhD-to-product transition
**Right**: Brief positioning that turns potential weakness into strength
